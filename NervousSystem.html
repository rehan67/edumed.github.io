Nervous system

What is the nervous system?

Nearly all of your actions, thoughts, words, and emotions are controlled by your nervous system. It manages complex functions like memory, thought, and movement. Additionally, it is crucial for bodily functions like breathing, blushing, and blinking that occur automatically.

what does the nervous system do?

Neurons are specialized cells that your nervous system uses to send signals or messages throughout your body. These electrical signals are sent and received by your muscles, organs, glands, skin, and brain. You can move your limbs and experience sensations like pain thanks to the messages. Information about your surroundings is taken in by your eyes, ears, tongue, nose, and all of the nerves throughout your body. Nerves then transfer that information to and from your brain. Signals sent by various neuronal types vary. Your muscles are told to move by motor neurons. Your brain receives signals from sensory neurons that process information from your senses. Other varieties of neurons manage automatic bodily functions like breathing, shivering, a regular heartbeat, and food digestion.

What are the parts of the nervous system?

Central Nervous System

Your CNS is made up of your brain and spinal cord. Your nerves are used by your brain to communicate with the rest of your body. A layer of protection called myelin surrounds each nerve. Myelin protects the nerve and aids in the transmission of signals.

Peripheral Nervous System

Your body's peripheral nervous system is made up of numerous nerves that branch off from your central nervous system (CNS). Your organs, arms, legs, fingers, and toes receive information from your brain and spinal cord through this system.

Diseases of Nervous system:

Stroke, Accedintal injury, Pressure, Aging process and Toxic substances
