Urinary system

Urinary or Renal system consists of numerous organs and muscles make up the urinary system, which produces, stores, and excretes urine. Waste products are the only things left over after your body digests food and liquids to absorb their nutrients. Urine is one of them. The liquid waste that results from the breakdown of protein-containing foods is known as Urea .

Urea is one of your body's main waste products and becomes urine when combined with water and other minerals to help keep the balance of chemicals and water in your body, your urinary system filters waste while communicating constantly with other organs.

The organs of the urinary system:

Kidney:
You have two kidneys from birth. Each of them is a bean-shaped organ that is about the size of a closed fist. Your kidneys, which are situated just below either side of your rib cage, are always at work. The nephrons in the kidneys function as a microscopic filtering system to remove urea from the blood. Additionally, they filter extraneous chemicals, ions, and other metabolic waste. Your kidneys will filter about 120 to 150 quarts of blood every single day, regardless of what you're doing.
Ureters
The ureters transport the filtered urine from the kidneys to the bladder. A ureter connects each kidney to the bladder, which is almost constantly emptied of minute amounts of urine. One of the reasons you should avoid holding your urine for an extended period of time is because movement is crucial to prevent infection in the ureters.
Bladder
Urine that has been emptied into the bladder sits there until you need to urinate. In order to match urine output, your bladder, which is situated in your lower abdomen, can impressively contract and expand. You can urinate when you need to empty your bladder thanks to voluntary muscle control. The amount of water in the body, kidney output, and bladder size all affect how often you urinate. A typical bladder can hold 1.5 to 2 cups of urine for 2 to 5 hours on average.
Sphincter Muscles
Your bladder's sphincter muscles remain contracted to prevent leakage. They can close very tightly and are circular, almost like wrapping a rubber band around the opening of your bladder. If you have issues with incontinence, your sphincter muscles are probably weak.
Nerves of the bladder
Your bladder nerves are activated when you finally feel the need to urinate. These are what make you feel alert and indicate that you need to find a bathroom.
Urethra
Your urethra will carry your urine out of your body after you receive the signal that it's time to urinate. All of these processes are efficiently carried out by your body, but mistakes can still occur.

The Importance of the Urinary System

Your body can successfully filter blood through the urinary system, which also produces urine as waste material, stores it, and excretes it. But it does more than just that. As it works to preserve homeostasis and produce necessary hormones, the urinary tract system is a crucial component of your body's overall maintenance.

Diseases of the Urinary system are:

UTI , kidney stones, urinary retention , Urinary incontinence,BPH and Benign Prostitis
