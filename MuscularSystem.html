Muscular system

Muscles are structure that helps the human frame move. Numerous fibers make up the human muscular system, which ensures blood flow and proper posture. A person's body contains approximately 700 muscles, which make up 50% of their weight.Each individual organs made up of skeletal tissues, tendons, and blood vessels. Various organs also contain these tissues.

What are the function of the muscular system?

Several daily processes, such as maintaining posture, movement, blood circulation, respiration, digestion, and temperature regulation, are made possible by the muscular system.

what are the three types of muscles?

  1. We move our arms and legs with the help of voluntary muscles called skeletal muscles. They are controlled by the brain and attached to the skeleton. few examples of skeletal muscles are: triceps, quadriceps, and biceps
  2. smooth muscle can be found in organs like the stomach and intestines. which are involuntary. In order to move food through the digestive system, it contracts and relaxes.
  3. The heart contains cardiac muscle, which is also uncontrollable. To circulate blood throughout the body, it contracts and relaxes.
