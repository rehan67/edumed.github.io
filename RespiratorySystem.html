Respiratory system

What is the respiratory system?

The respiratory system aids in the removal of CO2 from your body and the delivery of O2. Your respiratory system includes your lungs which are shielded by your ribcage as they rest in your chest.Your health may suffer if your respiratory system isn't functioning properly because less oxygen will get to your lungs.

Parts of the respiratory system

  1. Nose
  2. Pharynx(throat)
  3. Larynx (voice box)
  4. Trachea (wind pipe)
  5. Bronchi (tracheal airways branching off to form smaller bronchioles)
  6. Lungs

How your respiratory system works

Your diaphragm, a flat muscle at the base of your chest, contracts to allow you to breathe in. The chest enlarges as a result, allowing air to enter.

Your mouth and nose are how you inhale and exhale air. Along the way, the air is warmed and moistened.

Your larynx, which houses the vocal cords that enable you to speak, is where the air passes through.

When air reaches your lungs, it travels through the upper airways, which include the trachea (windpipe) and bronchi. Mucus is produced by the respiratory tract's lining to catch foreign objects.

Air resides in tiny air sacs in your lungs called alveoli, which are close to blood vessels. Your alveoli transport oxygen from the air you breathe into your blood. The opposite is true for carbon dioxide, which leaves your bloodstream and enters your alveoli. After that, you exhale the carbon dioxide.

Diseases of the respiratory system are:

Ashtma,bronchitis, COPD, Hay fever, Laryngitis,Pnemonia,Lung conditions,Whooping cough,Croup and Bronchiolitis
