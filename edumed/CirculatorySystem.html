CIRCULATORY SYSTEM

Your circulatory system, also referred to as your cardiovascular system, is a collection of organs that work in unison to perform a crucial task. It is responsible for getting oxygen and nutrients to all body organs and for removing waste products like carbon dioxide and toxins from the body. It is made up of the heart, vessels, and lymphatic circulation. Numerous conditions, including high blood pressure, atherosclerosis, angina, arrhythmia,blood clots, and varicose veins, can impair the circulatory system's ability to function properly. You can prevent these conditions by being aware of them.

WHAT IS THE CIRCULATORY SYSTEM?

The heart and blood vessels, through which blood travels, form an amazing network. Our organs receive oxygenated blood and nutrients from these vessels, which also help remove waste materials and carbon dioxide from the tissues and organs. By performing this exchange, the circulatory system aids in maintaining the health and life of our cells. The circulatory system understands how hard it needs to pump blood by listening to all the signals sent by the body's various organs. For life to exist, the circulatory system is necessary.

PARTS OF THE CIRCULATORY SYSTEM

The circulatory system is made up of numerous components, each of which performs a distinct task to keep the body in good health. The heart, the arteries, the veins, and the capillaries are the four components of this intricate system. Your heart can miraculously pump up to five liters (1.3 gallons) of blood per minute. And if you assume a resting heart rate of between 60 and 100 beats per minute, it will have beat up to 2.5 billion times by the time you are 70 years old.

THE HEART

The heart is the center of the circulatory system. You can fully understand the entire circulatory system when you comprehend this strong muscle and its operations. Because it is situated in the middle of the circulatory system, this organ is unique. It is constructed of numerous layers.

The pericardium is the wrap around the heart that keeps it in its place. It contains fluids that act as lubricants preventing friction of the cavities. The pericardium itself is made of two layers: The visceral layer that directly covers the heart, and the parietal layer that is the sac containing the fluid.

HOW DOES YOUR CIRCULATORY SYSTEM WORK?

The sole purpose of your heart is to pump blood into your circulatory system. Contraction and relaxation, which typically occur back-to-back, are the cause of this. Systole and diastole are the terms used to describe contraction and relaxation, respectively. The rhythmic patterns of these two allow the blood to be pumped. Thus, they combine to create the cardiac cycle, also known as the heartbeat, which lasts for about one second.
