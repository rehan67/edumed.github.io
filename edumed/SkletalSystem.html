Skeletal system

What is the Skeletal system?

All of the bones, cartilage, tendons, and ligaments in the human body make up the skeletal system. A person's skeleton makes up about 20% of their total body weight, according to a reliable source.

The 206 bones in an adult's skeleton are number. Because some of the bones, such as those in the skull, fuse together as people age, children's skeletons actually have more bones than adults.The skeletons of men and women differ in a few other ways as well. The average male skeleton is longer and has more bone mass. Contrarily, the female skeleton has a wider pelvis to allow for pregnancy and childbirth.

The skeletal system can be divided into two parts, known as the axial skeleton and the appendicular skeleton, regardless of age or gender.

Axial skeleton anatomy

There are 80 bones in the axial skeleton of an adult. It is composed of the bones that make up the body's vertical axis, including those in the head, neck, chest, and spine.

22 Skull bones

  • cranial bones: skull's 8 cranial bones make up the majority of it. They assist in defending your brain.
  • facial bones The 14 facial bones are counted. They make up the face and are located on the front of the skull.

Auditory ossicles

The inner ear canal of the skull contains six tiny bones called the auditory ossicles. On each side of the head, there are three auditory ossicles that Together, they enable sound waves from the environment to reach the inner ear structures.

  1. malleus

Hyoid

a U-shaped bone that can be found at the jaw's base. It functions as a neck joint where ligaments and muscles attach.

Vertebral column

There are 26 bones in the vertebral column. All 24 of the first vertebrae are then followed by the sacrum and coccyx (tailbone).

  1. cervical vertebrae: The head and neck contain these seven bones.
  2. Thoracic vertebrae: The upper back contains these 12 bones.
  3. Lumbar vertebrae: The lower back contains these five bones.

Boththe sacrum and the coccyx are constructed from a number of fused vertebrae. They aid in supporting the body's weight while seated. They also act as points of attachment for different ligaments.

Thoracic cage

The sternum (breastbone) and 12 pairs of ribs make up the thoracic cage. The upper torso organs, such as the heart and lungs, are encased in a cage made of these bones. Some of the ribs connect to the sternum directly, while others do so via cartilage. Some are referred to as "floating ribs" because they have no points of attachment.

Appendicular skeleton anatmoy

The appendicular skeleton contains 126 bones in total. Along with the bones that connect them to the axial skeleton, it is made up of the bones that make up the arms and legs.

Pectoral girdle

The arms connect to the axial skeleton at the pectoral girdle. The clavicle (collarbone) and scapula make up its structure (shoulder blade). Each of these comes in pairs, one for each arm.

Upper limb

  • The humerus is the long bone of the upper arm.
  • The radius is one of two long bones of the forearm, found on the thumb side.
  • The ulna is the second long bone of the forearm, found on the pinky finger side.
  • The carpals are a group of eight bones found in the wrist area.
  • The metacarpals are five bones found in the middle area of the hand.
  • The phalanges are 14 bones that make up the fingers.

Pelvic gridle

The legs connect to the axial skeleton at the pelvic girdle, also referred to as the hips. There are two hipbones in it, one for each leg.

  3. Pubis

What is the function of the skeletal system?

preventing damage to internal organs For instance, the thoracic cage protects the heart and lungs while the skull shields the brain, allowing for movement. Tendons allow muscles to connect to bones. This link enables the body to move in a variety of ways, including the production of blood cells. Numerous bones contain soft bone marrow, which makes platelets, white blood cells, and red blood cells in addition to storing minerals and nutrients. Calcium and phosphorus, two minerals that are crucial for numerous bodily processes, can be stored and released by bones. A portion of the bone marrow also contains adipose (fat) tissue that can be used as energy.
